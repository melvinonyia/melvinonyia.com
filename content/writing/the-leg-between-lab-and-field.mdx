A finding from a lab paper has to travel a long way before it changes what a
coach actually does on a Tuesday morning. The chain of custody is brittle:
the analysis lives in a notebook somewhere, the visualization is figure 3a
in a PDF, the conclusion is a sentence in the abstract, the practitioner
reads the sentence and has to decide what to do with their session plan.

The leg I care most about is the one between the analysis and the workflow
the practitioner already has open. Not the dashboard nobody opens; the field
the strength coach already types into. Most of the engineering work I do
sits on that leg, and the failure mode is almost always the same: the
software was designed for the question the researcher asked, not for the
decision the practitioner is about to make.

The shortest fix is usually subtractive — fewer numbers, fewer
configurations, fewer screens. The next-shortest is to put the model's
answer one click away from the next thing the practitioner is going to do
anyway. The longest, most rewarding fix is to redesign the model itself
around the decision instead of around the dataset.

A short list of things I keep coming back to is forming. I'll publish it
here when it's done.
