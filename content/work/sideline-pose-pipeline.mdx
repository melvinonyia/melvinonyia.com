## The problem

Markered motion capture is precise and unhelpful — it lives in a lab the
athlete only visits twice a season. The interesting data is the training day
they actually have. Sideline video is the only sensor the staff is willing to
set up, and the pipeline has to do the rest.

## What I built

A markerless pose pipeline that ingests sideline H.264 streams, runs per-frame
keypoint detection through a quantized PyTorch model, and emits per-athlete
pose tracks on a 60 ms end-to-end budget. The hot path is split across a small
fleet of T4s; cold storage and analysis batches share the same schema
downstream so notebooks and dashboards read the same data.

## Outcome

Two collegiate squads are running the pipeline against their training-room
dashboards. The performance staff can compare a day's session against an
athlete's own baseline before the athlete has left the room — closing a loop
that used to take a week.
